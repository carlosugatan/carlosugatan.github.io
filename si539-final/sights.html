Sights in Guam

Historical sights and landmarks are spread across Guam. From Latte Stones huts, forts, and monuments: visiting each site may take you half the day! It's always a great idea to schedule an "Around The Island" tour in your travel plan! Don't worry, this will only take a day! Starting from the North and ending the day in the South to enjoy the sunset. Nothing sounds better than that.

Fort Nuestra Senora de la Soldedadmore_vert
Fort Nuestra Senora de la Soldedadclose

Fort Nuestra Senora de la Soledad is a fortification near Umatac, Guam. It was built by the Spanish between 1802 and 1819, it was the fourth of four fortifications that protected an anchorage for galleons transiting between Acapulco, Mexico and the Philippines, a route that fell out of use in 1815 with Mexican independence (Wikipedia).

Tailafak Bridgemore_vert
Tailafak Bridgeclose

The Talifak Bridge was one of many small bridges along the El Camio Real, the old Spanish Coastal Road running from Hagatna to Umatac over which the Spanish transported supplies. Constructed in 1785, the Tailafak Bridge is one of the few original examples of Spanish architecture that still stand in their original locations (Guam Online).

The Plaza de Espana, located in central Hagatna, served as the Governor’s Palace during the Spanish, American and Japanese occupations, from 1734 until 1944 when when most of the buildings were destroyed during the shelling of Hagatna during the retaking of Guam in World War II (Guam Online.

Latte Stone Parkmore_vert
Latte Stone Parkclose

Latte Stones are pillars on which ancient Chamorro houses were constructed as early as 500 A.D. Found nowhere else in the world, Latte Stones have become a signature of Guam and the Northern Marianas Islands. This park located in Hagatna, Guam (Capital) but other Latte Stones are scattered all over the island. Can you count how many you see?
